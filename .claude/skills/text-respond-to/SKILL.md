# Respond To

Generate clear, effective responses to messages with a human, helpful, and authentic tone.

## Core Principles

**Tone**: Professional but warm and accessible
- Sound like a real person, not a corporate template
- Be helpful and genuine
- Use natural language

**Clarity**: Simple and direct
- Use accessible language
- Get to the point quickly
- Avoid jargon and corporate clichés

**Effectiveness**: Minimize back-and-forth
- Provide complete information upfront when possible
- Anticipate follow-up questions
- Be specific about next steps or actions
- Set clear expectations

**Brevity**: Keep it concise
- Avoid unnecessary elaboration
- Respect the recipient's time
- Don't pad responses with filler

## Process

1. **Understand the message**: Identify what type of message this is (question, favor request, news, update, etc.)

2. **Assess context needs**: If you need additional context to craft an appropriate response, ask the user:
   - "How do you want to respond?" (yes/no, providing info, declining, acknowledging, etc.)
   - Any relevant constraints (deadlines, capacity, priorities)
   - Relationship context if it affects tone
   - Any specific information to include

3. **Craft the response** following these patterns:

   **For questions**:
   - Answer directly and completely
   - Provide relevant context or caveats
   - Suggest next steps if applicable

   **For favor requests**:
   - Acknowledge the request clearly
   - Give a direct yes/no (or conditional)
   - Explain constraints if declining or conditionally accepting
   - Offer alternatives when saying no

   **For shared news**:
   - Acknowledge specifically what was shared
   - Share genuine reaction or thoughts
   - Ask relevant follow-up questions if appropriate

   **For updates/FYIs**:
   - Acknowledge receipt and understanding
   - Note any actions you'll take
   - Ask clarifying questions if needed

4. **Review**: Ensure the response is:
   - Human and authentic (not robotic)
   - Free of corporate speak ("circle back", "touch base", "synergize", etc.)
   - Concise but complete
   - Clear about next steps or expectations

## Examples

**Avoiding corporate clichés**:
- ❌ "Let's circle back on this"
- ✅ "Let's discuss this next week"

- ❌ "I'll loop you in going forward"
- ✅ "I'll keep you updated"

- ❌ "Let me touch base with the team"
- ✅ "I'll check with the team"

**Being concise but complete**:
- ❌ "Thank you so much for reaching out about this! I really appreciate you thinking of me..."
- ✅ "Thanks for thinking of me for this..."

**Being specific**:
- ❌ "I'll get back to you soon"
- ✅ "I'll have an answer by Friday"
